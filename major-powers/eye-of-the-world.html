Eye of the World

“Where the Eye gazes, truth breaks.”
— Fragment from a salvaged Selunean hymn

Overview

Once the heart of Selune, the Eye of the World was a towering arcane structure seated on the circular island at the center of the crescent-shaped Selune. Believed to predate even the Kingdom of Selune itself, the tower was more than a seat of power—it was a trial, a beacon, and a containment vessel for the darkness that stirred beneath the island.

The Eye collapsed centuries ago when the island of Selune fell into chaos and was lost to the world. Its fall ripped open the veil between realms, letting in monsters, corrupted magic, and artifacts from beyond the known world.

Original Purpose

Only in the final years before the fall was the Eye’s true nature rediscovered:

  • 🔺 A Trial of Succession:
    The tower was not simply a royal symbol—it was a test. Deep within its halls lay a series of arcane and moral trials, meant to judge and prepare a candidate to become the Vessel, a ruler tasked with absorbing and containing the darkness below Selune.

  • 🌀 An Arcane Beacon:
    The tower channeled leyline energy through Selune. Scholars believe it maintained magical equilibrium between the material plane and something outside it.

  • 🔒 A Seal:
    The Throne of Sealing, located at the tower's summit, was both a crown and a cage. Each Vessel who sat upon it bound themselves to the darkness, delaying its escape through self-sacrifice and strength of will.

Collapse

The final Vessel, a once-noble king, was corrupted by the very force he was meant to contain.
Rather than dying to protect Selune, he turned on his people, shattered the tower’s inner wards, and helped bring about the island’s destruction.

The collapse:

  • Tore open rifts between planes
  • Released monsters and magic not seen in the world before
  • Caused Selune’s total isolation, leaving it unreachable by magic or sea

The Final Attempt

In the final years before Selune’s fall, the truth about the Trial of the Eye became known. One last group of adventurers, renowned across the continent, attempted to ascend the tower and seal the darkness anew:

  • Rune, a warforged mage whose very design echoed the lost machines of Balance
  • Valira, a tiefling warlock scorned by society, but gifted in weaving truths from the Void
  • Athena, a human paladin whose faith had once caused monsters to flee at her presence
  • And a fourth, never named in any surviving record—some say by choice, others by necessity

They failed. Whether they fell during the trials, were betrayed by the throne, or simply could not reach the summit in time is unknown. But their attempt marks the last known act of resistance before the tower was consumed by darkness.

Modern Legacy

The Shattered Sight Cult

  • Founded by a mage who came into contact with the broken throne long after the collapse.
  • This survivor claimed the Eye whispered truths that no mind was meant to hold.
  • A rift-born monster confirmed the visions before its death, giving rise to a fanatic belief that the Eye was divine.

“We are not meant to seal the Eye. We are meant to be seen by it.”

🔍 Relics & Fragments

  • A few fragments of tower, etched with runes that reshape when not looked at, have appeared in the hands of collectors, heretics, and the Delyssar.
  • One cultist’s blade, said to be forged from throne-shard metal, has the ability to "remember" each person it kills—growing heavier with each soul.

Rumors & Mysteries

  • The throne at the tower’s peak still exists, broken but not powerless.
  • Some say the Eye was never meant to contain evil, but to draw it in like a lure for something greater.
  • Certain storm-wracked nights bring echoes of Selune’s final cry to distant shores.
  • Obelion wants access to surviving throne fragments—but Myriath forbids it.
