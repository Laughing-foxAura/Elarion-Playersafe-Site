🌍 The World of Elarion

A single-continent world with two distant islands, fractured kingdoms, magical secrets, and a growing threat from the north.

Kingdoms of the Continent

Virelia

  • Type: Centralized monarchy
  • Ruler: Queen Araleth VI
  • Capital: Caer Thalor
  • Magic: Strictly regulated; only licensed practitioners
  • Military: Highly disciplined and loyal
  • Nobility: Most noble houses are loyal to the queen

Dravandor Confederacy

  • Type: Alliance of free city-states
  • Council Head: Lord Berrick of Myrhold
  • Major Cities: Myrhold, Tarnath, Velmira, Cenwyn, Estrellan
  • Magic: Lightly regulated, magic-users live freely
  • Military: Independent militias and hired adventurers

Arken Dominion (Historical)

  • Type: Theocratic empire
  • Ruler: Emperor Kavalh
  • Capital: Varkhaz-Thul (surrounded by rivers of lava)
  • Magic: Only divine and state-sanctioned arcane
  • Known for: Master forges powered by volcanic flows

Valewind Pactlands

  • Type: Druidic confederation

  • Council: Council of Speakers

  • Magic: Natural and spiritual magic, tied to leylines

  • Known for: Deep forests, harmony with nature

  • Obelion floats above Valewind, strengthening the leyline beneath it.

Myriath

  • Type: Independent holy city-state
  • Governance: Council of Halos
  • Religion: Center of the Church that worships Saint Vivi
  • Known for: The Perfect Barrier that repels evil
  • The Barrier protects the entire north.
  • Saint Vivi is revered as a martyr who died casting the Barrier.

Thalor Sylvae

  • Type: Elven forest kingdom
  • Ruler: Queen Liraethe Velyndor
  • Magic: Ancient, dream-bound, and song-based
  • Culture: Reverent, quiet, rarely seen beyond the woods

Neutral Powers

Obelion

  • Type: Floating arcane citadel (Known as: The One True Tower)
  • Governance: Archmage and Circle of Twelve
  • Location: Mostly hovers above Valewind
  • Known for: Research into barriers, leylines, and forgotten magic
  • Obelion has discovered how to move the floating island, albeit slowly.

Adventurer’s Guild

  • HQ: Steelmark, an independent city not claimed by any kingdom
  • Structure: Strict rank-based system:
    • Porcelain, Copper, Bronze, Iron, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Mithril, Orichalcum, Adamantite
    • Mithril and above require recommendation and legendary deeds
  • Officially neutral and does not participate in wars.

Other Regions

Selune

  • Former crescent-shaped island kingdom
  • Now a ruined landmass overrun by monsters and rift-magic
  • At its center stood the Eye of the World—once a trial site for a vessel meant to contain evil
History

In the previous campaign, the players failed to complete the trial at the Eye. The ancient king who once absorbed evil was corrupted, causing the island’s collapse and unleashing chaos.

Monster Realm

  • Location: Beyond the Titan’s Spine Mountains in the north

  • Believed to be a result of Selune’s collapse and planar rift magic

World Rules

Healing and Magic Rules

  • Magic is rare. Only the Gifted can level up or cast spells.
  • Healing magic is incredibly uncommon outside of the Church, elves, and adventurers.
  • Potions only work for the Gifted.

Magic Items and Tools

  • Magic items (wands, weapons, tools, etc.) can be used by anyone, even those without the Gift.
  • This makes enchanted tools and relics incredibly valuable, especially to nobles, military forces, and wealthy tradesfolk.
  • Most magic tools are crafted with specific intent—many require training, activation rituals, or command words passed down through bloodlines or guilds.
  • Powerful artifacts still require attunement by a Gifted soul.

Rifts in the World

“The world isn’t breaking. It’s remembering.”
— Unknown bard, one night before vanishing near the Shoreline

Overview

In recent years, strange phenomena have begun to surface across the land—glimmering distortions, patches of withered earth, places where time moves oddly. Some call them rifts. Others whisper of cracks in the world.

No one knows their true origin.

  • Scholars speculate they may be remnants of ancient magic.
  • Some blame the fall of Selune, the lost island that once held the Eye of the World.
  • Others claim the world is simply unraveling—slowly, and with purpose.

What People Know

  • Rifts appear in places of old power: forgotten ruins, ancient battlefields, leyline nexuses, or cursed groves.
  • They are often harmless to observe, though deeply unsettling:
    • Colors shift,
    • Sound warps,
    • Gravity misbehaves.
  • Magic acts unpredictably near them.
    Spells might flare, fizzle, or echo back twisted versions of themselves.
  • Most rifts attract strange creatures. Others do nothing—for years.
  • Tales persist of voices heard through rifts—familiar, dead, or never known.

Current Beliefs

  • The Church:
    • Cautious and divided. Some clergy call rifts divine trials; others fear corruption.
  • The Adventurer’s Guild:
    • Monitors and classifies rift zones.
    • Bounties exist for rift investigation, recovery, or neutralization.
  • The Magic Tower of Obelion:
    • Publicly silent.
    • Rumored to secretly observe, catalog, and experiment with rifts.
  • Common Folk:
    • Avoid rifts at all costs.
    • Build shrines, wards, or stone circles nearby—just in case.

Known Rift Sites

Cenwyn
A rural city where sudden auroras and eerie rainfall are becoming common.
Locals report strange dreams, missing livestock, and lights in the woods.

Obsidian Shoreline
An abandoned port town where a rift shimmers offshore.
Fishermen claim something watches from the sea floor.

The Withered Hollow
A once-lush grove now completely dead.
No birds sing, and tools rust overnight when left in the clearing.

Common Rumors

  • Passing through a rift may change a person—body or soul.
  • Artifacts found near rifts sometimes exhibit sentience or warped magic.
  • Some bards sing of a time before the rifts, when the world was whole.
